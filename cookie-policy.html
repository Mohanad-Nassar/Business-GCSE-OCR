In short

This site does not use tracking or advertising cookies, and we don't show a cookie banner — because everything we store on your device is strictly necessary to log you in and save your quiz progress, which the law says doesn't need a consent pop-up.

1. What we actually use

We don't set traditional browser cookies for tracking. Instead we use your browser's local storage for a small number of strictly functional items:

ItemPurposeKept until
gcse_session_v1Keeps you logged in between visits. You log out, or your session expires.
Per-topic progress cache (geo_progress_… keys) Saves your in-progress quiz answers on your device so you don't lose your place, and syncs with the server copy of your answer history. Cleared if you reset a topic, or clear your browser data.
Small gamification flags (badges seen, sound on/off, celebration-shown markers) Remembers small display preferences, e.g. not re-showing a badge popup you've already seen.Cleared if you clear your browser data.

2. Why no consent banner?

Under the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR), a cookie/storage consent banner is only required for things that are not strictly necessary for the service you've asked for (e.g. advertising or analytics cookies). Everything listed above exists purely to make the revision tool itself work — staying logged in and not losing your progress — so it falls under the "strictly necessary" exemption and no banner is shown.

3. Third-party requests

This page's fonts are loaded from Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com and fonts.gstatic.com). Loading a webpage that references Google Fonts means your browser makes a direct request to Google's servers, which will see your device's IP address as part of that request. Google Fonts does not set tracking cookies in this configuration, but this is disclosed here for transparency.

4. No analytics or advertising

We do not use Google Analytics, Meta/Facebook Pixel, or any other analytics or advertising tracking script. If that ever changes, this policy will be updated first and a consent banner will be added before any non-essential tracking goes live.

5. Managing or clearing this data

You can clear local storage at any time through your browser's settings (usually under "Privacy" or "Clear browsing data"). Doing so will log you out and clear any locally-cached quiz progress — your answer history already saved to the server is not affected and will resync next time you log in.
