In short

We keep a note of the username you log in with, the answers you give to revision questions (so we can show you what to practise next), and how many days in a row you've used the site. We don't collect your real name, and we don't share your data with anyone except the companies that host the site for us. You or your teacher/parent can ask to see or delete your data at any time.

1. Who we are

This site (the "Platform") is a revision tool for OCR GCSE Business Studies, built and run by a teacher at a school within Avanti Schools Trust. Until formal Trust adoption is complete, data protection queries should be sent to mohanadmaeen.edu@gmail.com, marked "Data Protection Query". Once adopted, this section will be updated with the Trust's registered Data Protection Officer.

3. Why we collect it

  • Educational tracking: so you and your teacher can see which topics you've mastered and which need more revision.
  • Formative assessment: teachers use class-level dashboards to identify gaps and set follow-up tasks.
  • Motivation: XP, levels, badges and streaks are shown to encourage regular, healthy revision habits — see our Children's Code statement for how we keep this age-appropriate and non-manipulative.

4. Lawful basis

We process pupil data on the basis of the school's public task in delivering education and legitimate interests in supporting revision and formative assessment. Teacher account data is processed under legitimate interests in running the Platform. This basis will be formally confirmed as part of the Trust's Data Protection Impact Assessment sign-off.

5. How long we keep it

Student accounts and associated progress data are kept for 12 months after your last recorded activity, or 12 months after you leave the school — whichever is later — after which they are deleted or anonymised. Note: as of this policy's last update, the automated process that carries this out has not yet been built; until it is, deletion happens manually on request. This is tracked as an open action in the Platform's DPIA.

6. Where your data is stored and how it's protected

  • Data is hosted with Supabase (database and authentication) and Netlify (website hosting), both of whom act as our data processors. Both are confirmed to host this project's data in a UK/EU region.
  • All connections use HTTPS encryption in transit.
  • Database access is controlled with row-level security, so a student can only ever read or write their own records, and a teacher can only see students in their own classes.
  • No advertising or analytics companies have access to your data.

7. Who we share your data with

We do not sell or share your data for marketing purposes. Data is only accessible to:

  • Your teacher(s), for classes you're enrolled in.
  • Our hosting sub-processors (Supabase, Netlify), strictly to run the service.
  • The Trust/school, if and when this system is formally adopted and integrated with existing safeguarding and data-protection oversight.

8. International transfers

As of this policy's last update, both Supabase and Netlify host this Platform's data in the UK/EU, so no international transfer safeguards are currently required. If this changes, this policy will be updated to describe the safeguards used (such as the UK's International Data Transfer Agreement).

9. Cookies and local storage

The Platform uses your browser's local storage (not tracking cookies) to keep you logged in and to save quiz progress between visits. See our Cookie Policy for full details — none of this is used for advertising or analytics.

10. Your rights

You (or, for younger pupils, a parent/carer or your teacher on your behalf) can ask us to:

  • Show you the personal data we hold about you (right of access).
  • Correct inaccurate data (right of rectification).
  • Delete your data, subject to any legal/educational record-keeping requirements (right of erasure).
  • Restrict or object to certain processing.

To make a request, email mohanadmaeen.edu@gmail.com. You also have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) if you think your data has been mishandled.

11. Children using this Platform

This Platform is used by pupils aged approximately 11–16 (Key Stage 3 and 4). We follow the ICO's Age Appropriate Design Code — see our dedicated Children's Code page for a plain-English explanation.

12. Changes to this policy

We'll update this page if what we collect or why changes, and update the "last updated" date at the top. Significant changes will be flagged to teachers to pass on to pupils/parents.
