1. Sales Revenue

The total amount of money coming into the business from the sale of goods or services.

Formula: Total Sales Revenue = Selling Price × Quantity Sold

2. Total Variable Costs

Costs that change directly with the level of output or sales.

Formula: Total Variable Costs = Variable Cost (per unit) × Quantity

3. Total Costs

The entire cost of running the business, combining fixed costs (which do not change with output) and total variable costs.

Formula: Total Costs = Total Variable Costs + Total Fixed Costs

4. Cash Flow

The flow of money into and out of the business over a period of time.

Net Cash Flow = Total Inflow - Total Outflow
Closing Balance = Net Cash Flow + Opening Balance

5. Gross Profit

The profit made after subtracting the direct costs of making the product (Cost of Sales) from the Sales Revenue.

Formula: Gross Profit = Sales Revenue - Cost of Sales

6. Gross Profit Margin

This shows the percentage of sales revenue that is kept as gross profit. A higher percentage is better.

Formula: Gross Profit Margin = (Gross Profit ÷ Sales Revenue) × 100

7. Net Profit

The final profit made after ALL expenses (like rent, salaries, and marketing) have been deducted from the Gross Profit.

Formula: Net Profit = Gross Profit - Expenses
(OR Net Profit = Total Revenue - Total Costs)

8. Net Profit Margin

This shows the final percentage of revenue that is actual profit after everything is paid for. It is the best measure of overall efficiency.

Formula: Net Profit Margin = (Net Profit ÷ Sales Revenue) × 100

9. Break-Even Quantity

The point where a business makes neither a profit nor a loss (Total Revenue = Total Costs). First find the contribution per unit, then divide fixed costs by that contribution.

Formula: Fixed Costs ÷ (Selling Price - Variable Cost per unit)

10. Average Rate of Return (ARR)

A method used to compare the profitability of different investments over their lifetime.

Unless the average return for the investment is already known, the following three stepped approach is needed to calculate the ARR:

  1. Calculate the total profit from an investment over its lifetime.
    Formula: Total income received from an investment over a given period of time - cost of the investment
  2. Calculate the average profit from an investment per year.
    Formula: Total profit from the investment ÷ Number of years
  3. Calculate the average rate of return from an investment.
    Formula: (Average profit from the investment ÷ Cost of the investment) × 100
