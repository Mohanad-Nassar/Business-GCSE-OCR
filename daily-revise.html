ℹ️ How Daily Revise works — and how you earn coins

Mastering a question: the Rule of 3

A question isn't finished the first time you get it right. You have to answer that same question correctly three separate times, spread over days, before it counts as mastered. (It's three goes at one question — not three different questions.)

  1. 1stCorrect — the question comes back in a day or so.
  2. 2ndCorrect again — it returns once more, further apart.
  3. 3rdCorrect a third time — mastered. It stops appearing in your daily set.

Get it wrong at any point and the count goes back to zero. That's deliberate — anything you've forgotten comes back until it genuinely sticks. It's not a punishment, and it never takes coins away.

Earning coins ✦

You get paid as you learn — each correct answer of the same question is worth more than the last:

  • 1st time correct → 1 ✦
  • 2nd time correct → 2 ✦
  • 3rd time correct (mastered) → 3 ✦

That's 6 coins for every question you master. Wrong answers earn nothing, but they never cost you anything either. Spend what you earn in The Locker 🛍️.

You also earn 5 ✦ for completing a review, and 10 ✦ for completing a task your teacher set — paid when you submit it, whatever you score.

For teachers

Mastery is tracked per question, per student: the count rises on a correct answer and resets to zero on a wrong one, so "mastered" reflects genuine retention across spaced repetitions rather than one lucky guess. Questions a student has mastered drop out of the daily queue; forgotten ones re-surface automatically.

Coins are computed on the server from that same signal and banked in a ledger that never decreases — so re-climbing a ladder a student has already been paid for earns nothing. There is no way to farm coins by deliberately answering wrong and re-answering, and a daily cap bounds how fast coins can build up. Coins are cosmetic-only: they never affect marks, mastery, or your reports.
