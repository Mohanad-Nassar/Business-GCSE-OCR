In short

Use this site to revise honestly, keep your login to yourself, and don't try to mess with the scoring or other people's accounts. Breaking these rules can get an account suspended, and anything that raises a safeguarding concern will be passed to your school's pastoral team.

1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy sets out the standard of behaviour expected of anyone using the Platform, and the actions we may take if it isn't followed.

2. Expected behaviour

  • Use the Platform for genuine revision of OCR GCSE Business Studies content.
  • Keep your username/email and password private — don't share your login with classmates.
  • Only access your own account and your own progress data.
  • Report anything that looks broken, unfair, or unsafe to a teacher or via the contact details below.

3. Prohibited actions

  • Attempting to access another student's or teacher's account or data.
  • Attempting to tamper with, reverse-engineer, or bypass the scoring, XP, streak, or dashboard logic (for example, editing local storage to fake progress, or scripting/automating answer submission).
  • Attempting to bypass topic-access restrictions set by your teacher.
  • Sharing teacher invite codes with anyone not authorised by the school.
  • Submitting abusive, inappropriate, or unsafe content in any free-text answer field.
  • Any attempt to disrupt, overload, or gain unauthorised access to the Platform's systems.

4. Consequences

We may suspend or terminate any account found in breach of this policy, without notice where necessary to protect the Platform or other users. Where a breach also raises a safeguarding concern (for example, content suggesting a pupil is at risk of harm), this will be escalated to the school's designated safeguarding lead in line with normal school safeguarding procedures — this Platform does not replace or override the school's own safeguarding policy.

5. Reporting misuse

If you believe this policy has been breached, or you've spotted a way to tamper with the system, please report it responsibly to mohanadmaeen.edu@gmail.com rather than exploiting it.
