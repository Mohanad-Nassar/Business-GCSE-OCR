In short

We want every pupil to be able to use this revision site, whatever device or assistive technology they use. We aim to meet WCAG 2.2 AA, we know there are some gaps, and we want to hear about any problems you run into.

1. Our commitment

We want this Platform to be usable by as many pupils and teachers as possible, including those using screen readers, keyboard-only navigation, magnification, or other assistive technology. We aim to meet the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2, Level AA.

2. What's currently supported

  • Semantic HTML structure throughout topic pages, quizzes, and dashboards.
  • Keyboard navigation for interactive elements, including flashcards, quizzes, and tabbed sections, with a visible focus outline on every focusable element.
  • Colour choices intended to give reasonable contrast between text and background.
  • Descriptive labels on form fields (login, quiz answers) for screen-reader users.

3. Known limitations

This site has not yet had a full, independent accessibility audit. Known gaps we're aware of include:

  • Some drag-and-drop matching activities may be harder to complete with a keyboard alone or a screen reader than with a mouse/touchscreen.
  • Some diagrams/images used in case studies may not yet have full descriptive alt text.
  • Colour contrast has not been formally tested against every theme/colour combination used across all topic pages.

We're working through these as time allows, prioritising anything a pupil or teacher reports to us.

4. Reporting an accessibility problem

If you find something on this Platform that you can't access or use, please tell us at mohanadmaeen.edu@gmail.com. Please describe the page, what you were trying to do, and what assistive technology (if any) you were using — this is a teacher-run resource, so please allow a reasonable amount of time for a response and fix.

5. Reviewing this statement

This statement will be reviewed periodically, and formally reassessed once this Platform is adopted by Avanti Schools Trust.
