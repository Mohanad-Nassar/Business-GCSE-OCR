Command word & question-type patterns

Click any topic below to expand its full command word breakdown and examiner patterns. Understanding which command words appear with each topic is essential for targeted exam preparation.

2.4 The Marketing Mix
StateIdentifyAnalyseEvaluateRecommend
▾

Marks: 1, 2, 3, 6, 7★, 9★

The most-examined topic by a wide margin. Appears in every paper. Typically anchors Q16(f) or Q18(c) as the high-tariff evaluate question. Common angles: pricing methods (cost-plus, skimming, competitor), product (lifecycle, new product development), promotion (social media, TV, radio, newspaper, website), and place (distribution channels, store investment). The 7-mark Q16 evaluate has focused on marketing mix decisions in six of eight years (Sample, 2019, 2020 all had 7-mark 2.4 evaluates). In 2023 the 9★ Q18 also examined 2.4. The Recommend question in Q17 and Q18 part (c)(ii) almost always involves two promotional or pricing methods side by side.

3.4 Recruitment & Selection
StateIdentifyExplainAnalyseRecommend
▾

Marks: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9★

Always appears as a multi-part question covering selection methods (interviews, references, group activities) alongside a Recommend sub-part. The 9-mark starred Q18 focused on recruitment in 2020. In shorter Q16/Q17 slots it typically earns 2 or 3 marks (State two methods, Explain one advantage of external recruitment). Job descriptions and person specifications featured in the Sample paper only.

3.6 Training & Development
IdentifyAnalyseExplainEvaluate
▾

Marks: 2, 3, 6, 7★

Common in both short-answer (Analyse one reason / Identify two other methods) and as the 7-mark evaluate (2022 Q16: evaluate apprenticeships). Training types examined include induction, on-the-job, off-the-job, apprenticeships, and academic qualifications. Almost always linked to a case study business with specific operational context (e.g. customer service training, staff development programme).

3.5 Motivation & Retention
StateIdentifyExplainAnalyseEvaluate
▾

Marks: 1, 2, 3, 6, 7★

Has been the 7-mark Q16 Evaluate topic twice (2021: profit sharing; 2025: higher wage rate). Also appears frequently as Explain three reasons why employee motivation is important [6 marks]. Both financial (pay, profit sharing, bonuses) and non-financial (praise, autonomy) methods are regularly examined. Employee retention is a distinct sub-topic appearing in 2020, 2024, and 2025.

3.2 Organisational Structures & Ways of Working
StateExplainAnalyseRecommendDiscuss
▾

Marks: 2, 3, 6, 9★

Consistently examined through the structured Analyse one advantage of each option + Recommend format (e.g. tall vs flat, full-time vs part-time, work from home vs office). The 9-mark starred Q18 examined organisational structure in 2019. Ways of working (part-time, freelance, zero-hours) appear as State-type questions. The Q18 format of analysing two contrasting structures has appeared in 2021 (tall/flat), 2023 (working from home), and 2025 (full-time/part-time).

2.2 Market Research
StateIdentifyExplainAnalyseEvaluate
▾

Marks: 1, 2, 3, 6, 7★, 9★

Appears in every single year and has been both the 7-mark Q16 evaluate (2024: primary vs secondary) and the 9-mark Q18 starred question (2025: whether to use market research). Focus groups and questionnaires are the most commonly examined primary methods. Quantitative vs qualitative data distinction has appeared in 2024. Secondary sources (e.g. internet, government statistics) first appeared in 2019.

1.6 Business Growth
IdentifyExplainAnalyseEvaluateRecommend
▾

Marks: 1, 2, 3, 6, 7★, 9★

Has been the 7-mark evaluate in 2023 (organic growth) and the 9-mark starred in 2022 (merger decision). Organic vs external growth is the most consistent framing. Methods of external growth (merger, takeover, franchise) appear as Identify or Analyse questions. Absent in 2020 and 2024.

1.5 Stakeholders
IdentifyExplainAnalyseRecommend
▾

Marks: 1, 2, 3, 6

Has never been the highest-tariff question on its own. Appears as shorter questions (Identify one likely objective of an employee; Explain how three external stakeholder groups can be satisfied). Often combined with another topic in Q18 (e.g. stakeholder impact of a merger). Absent in both 2024 and 2025 — a significant two-year gap.

Either a short 2-mark Explain question or the full 9-mark starred Q18 evaluation (Sample 2018 and 2024). Never appeared as the 7-mark Q16 evaluate. Often framed around purpose/benefits of a business plan, or evaluating whether an existing or new plan is needed.

1.3 Business Ownership
ExplainAnalyseEvaluate
▾

Marks: 2, 6, 9★

Always involves private limited companies (Ltd) or sole traders — no plc-specific questions have used 1.3 as a primary tag despite the Sainsbury's context in 2024. The 9-mark starred Q18 examined sole trader vs alternative in 2021. Appeared in 2024 and 2025 consecutively.

2.3 Market Segmentation
StateExplainAnalyse
▾

Marks: 1, 2, 3, 6

Never appeared as a high-tariff evaluate or starred question. Always examined at the short-answer or analysis level (Explain one advantage / Analyse two benefits). Consistently links to demographic or geographic segmentation in case study context. Absent in 2022 and 2025.

3.3 Communication
IdentifyExplainAnalyseRecommend
▾

Marks: 1, 2, 3, 6

Has grown in prominence — appeared in 2019, 2021, 2023, 2024, 2025. In 2024 Q18 it featured as a structured Analyse-two-methods + Recommend format worth 9 marks in total. Methods examined include meetings, emails, and written communications. Appeared in Q18 in 2024 and 2025.

3.7 Employment Law
StateExplainAnalyse
▾

Marks: 1, 2, 3

Always a short-answer question — never examined at 6+ marks as a standalone topic. Anti-discrimination law (Equality Act context) and minimum wage are common angles. Appeared in Sample, 2020, 2021, and 2024 — skipped 2019, 2022, 2023, 2025.

2.4 — Marketing Mix 1
Analyse one advantage for [Business X] of using [pricing/promotion method A]. Analyse one advantage for [Business X] of using [pricing/promotion method B]. Recommend which method [Business X] should use. Give a reason for your answer.
6 + 3 marks  |  Command words: Analyse × 2, Recommend
Confidence
98%
▸ Why this topic?

2.4 has appeared in every one of the eight papers without exception, making it a near-certainty. The structured compare-two-methods + Recommend format has featured in Q17 or Q18 in 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023, 2024, and 2025. Given that 2.4 was not the 7-mark or 9-mark evaluate in 2025 (it was the Q17(c) 3-mark Analyse), there is a plausible case it returns as a higher-tariff question in 2026. Pricing and promotion are the most recurring sub-topics.

6 marks (Explain) or 7★/9★ (Evaluate)  |  Command words: Explain, Evaluate
Confidence
90%
▸ Why this topic?

1.5 appeared in six consecutive papers (Sample–2023) before going absent in both 2024 and 2025 — the longest gap in its history. It has never been the 9-mark starred question, making the combination of a long absence and an untested high-tariff format especially significant. The 2025 Q17(d) question on shareholder vs customer objectives (tagged 1.4) shows the examiner is interested in stakeholder tensions, reinforcing the likelihood that 1.5 returns in a more prominent role in 2026.

2 + 3 + 3 + 3 marks  |  Command words: State, Analyse, Recommend
Confidence
85%
▸ Why this topic?

3.4 has appeared in 7 of 8 papers (missing only 2022) and featured in both 2024 and 2025, confirming it as a near-annual topic. The structured analyse-two-methods + recommend format for selection methods is one of the most recognisable patterns in the paper, used in 2019, 2024, and 2025. It has also been the 9★ Q18 topic (2020), making it a plausible candidate at any mark allocation.

2 marks (State) or 3 + 3 + 3 (Analyse + Recommend)  |  Command words: State, Analyse, Recommend
Confidence
88%
▸ Why this topic?

2.2 has appeared in every paper. In 2024 it was the 7-mark Q16 Evaluate and in 2025 the 9-mark Q18 starred question — it therefore carried heavy tariff in two consecutive years. For 2026 it is likely to appear at a lower mark allocation (2-mark State or 3-mark Analyse), which still makes it a near-certainty but perhaps not the headline question. The quantitative vs qualitative data distinction (examined in 2024) is a likely re-examination angle.
